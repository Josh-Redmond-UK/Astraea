Geo-obfuscation of satellite imagery with diffusion models

A common concern raised by participants in my research, regardless of their background, was the potential danger of releasing satellite imagery which showed sensitive places or vulnerable people, even if individuals were not identifiable. Even the contextual clues of certain imagery being released as part of an investigation or project could be enough to risk harm.

Most approaches in the literature for dealing with sensitive information of this nature are social or organisational in their approach - such as sharing data with a network of trusted partners. These approaches assume a certain social situation and context which may not be available to all users.

The purpose of this research is to develop an additional technical approach to supplement these methods, and develop the concept of geo-obfuscation for satellite imagery.
